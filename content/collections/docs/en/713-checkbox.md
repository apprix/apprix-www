In the <b>Checkbox</b> element, the trainee is presented with several statements or questions at once, and the trainee chooses the correct option from two options (e.g. Yes/No, True/False, Do/Don’t). Each statement/question has its own answer text so you can fit a lot of content in the element.

One of the most common uses is the final test of a course. In the <b>Checkbox</b> element, you can use a larger pool of questions and randomly show only a few (<b>Shuffle</b> option).

From the <b>gear icon</b> in <b>Edit Module Contents</b> view, you may set the task questions or statements as correct or incorrect, add and delete them and change their order. 

<b>“Answer Mode” Options</b>

To activate the <b>Answer Mode</b> option, click the gear icon on the left side of the window.

<b>Option 1. The Answer Mode is switched off</b>
<ul>
 <li aria-level="1">Click each item tick box. An answer<b> text</b> pop-up window will appear.</li>
 <li aria-level="1">Edit its content.</li>
 <li aria-level="1">Write additional information about the topic in the statement here. Note that the content will be the same regardless of the given answer (e.g. True/False).</li>
</ul>
<b>Option 2: The Answer Mode is switched on</b>
<ul>
 <li aria-level="1">Switch on the <b>Answer Mode</b> by clicking the blue button on the Setting Bar.</li>
 <li aria-level="1">When you click on the item tick box, the Answer text pop-up window will open. At the top of the pop-up window, the <b>RIGHT</b> and <b>WRONG</b> options will appear. Type the answers for the different options.</li>
</ul>
<b>NOTE! </b>If the <b>Answer text </b>pop-up window is left empty, no pop-up window will be displayed after the employee has ticked the answer box.
