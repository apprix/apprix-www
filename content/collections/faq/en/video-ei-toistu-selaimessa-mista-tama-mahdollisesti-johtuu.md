title: 'A video does not play in the browser. What could be causing this?'
answer: '<p>If the video uses a non-standard resolution (e.g. 1936×1056) and a low frame rate (e.g. 9.602 fps), one or both of these may cause the video to not play in the browser.</p><p>Please ensure that the video used in the training plays normally in the browsers used by the participants.</p><p>For example, if participants are using Microsoft Edge, the video used in the video element should play normally when the video element is opened in preview mode in the Edge browser.</p><p>How do I check the video quality (resolution) and frame rate?</p><p>The video quality and frame rate can be checked using the VLC application (must be installed separately).</p><p>You can check the video quality and frame rate as follows</p><ul><li>Open the VLC application, select the video file by choosing Media > Open file from the top menu and then selecting the video to be used.</li><li>Select Tools > Media Information and then Codec from the window that opens</li><li>In the list in the view, the video quality should appear under Video resolution and the video frame rate under Frame rate.</li></ul><p>We recommend changing the video quality (resolution) to at least 1920×1080 and the frame rate to at least 30 fps (frames per second). This video quality is supported by almost all browsers. If you need help converting the video to a format that is better supported by browsers, Apprix HelpDesk is happy to help — please contact them. (helpdesk@apprix.fi)</p>'
