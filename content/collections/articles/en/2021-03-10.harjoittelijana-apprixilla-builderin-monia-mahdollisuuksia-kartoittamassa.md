        text: 'Now that I have worked with Builder more, its possibilities have become clearer to me. I have noticed that Builder offers great capabilities for any kind of training. Builder also makes it possible to train large groups, and the online course can be completed at a time that suits each individual – quite convenient in my opinion.'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'With the diverse range of interactive elements, both interactivity, inspiration and learner engagement are possible. In my view, the best part is that the trainee gets to actively participate and engage, rather than just sitting through an online lecture and listening. Some things can certainly be presented well through video, and Builder has that option too. For example, you can add a video in Builder and follow it with a few questions about the video''s topic. This way you engage the user and can ensure the video has been watched attentively.'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'Engaging the user is also easy through various task elements. In my opinion, a course becomes engaging almost automatically, as long as you remember to use elements diversely and include different types of tasks, such as true/false statements and multiple-choice questions. After the tasks, you immediately see the correct answer with explanations. I like this, because it supports learning.'

    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'Builder also enables data collection. For example, during onboarding you can survey perceptions of the employer or feelings about the first day at work. In sales training, you could ask how well the trainee knows the features of the products being sold.'

    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'As a content producer, I have encountered very few challenges with Builder, and for most puzzles that required thought, I received good insights and tips from our content production team. Occasionally, however, I have found it somewhat challenging to narrow down the material. When there is a lot of material, narrowing it down is important so that the course stays focused, remains motivating and does not stretch too long. Condensing is also a pedagogically sound approach.'
