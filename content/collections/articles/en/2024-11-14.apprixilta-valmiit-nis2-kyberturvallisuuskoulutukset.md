The revised cybersecurity directive sets new obligations for actors in both the public and private sectors. The NIS 2.0 directive was created to improve the EU''s ability to respond to increasingly complex and widespread cyber threats brought about by digitalisation and global connectivity. Its requirements emphasise a systematic approach to cybersecurity, which means, among other things, updating risk management practices, implementing proactive measures, and strengthening information security competence at all levels of the organisation.'

  -
    type: paragraph

      -
        type: text
        text: 'The scope of the NIS 2.0 directive has expanded compared to the original NIS directive. Previously, the regulations were mainly applied to critical infrastructure operators, such as companies in the energy, transport, and financial sectors. Now NIS 2.0 also obligates many other actors, such as healthcare providers, digital service providers, and manufacturing companies. This means that organisations must adopt more comprehensive cybersecurity practices and ensure that their staff have sufficient competence to identify and counter cyber threats.'
