        text: 'Companies repeatedly face the same problem: employees complete their assigned tasks, but an atmosphere that fosters efficiency and dynamism is nowhere to be found. Additionally, company values may fail to materialise at the grassroots level in daily work. So where can one find a new boost for the work community? An enlightened employer knows that the answer would certainly be found by listening to employees. However, there is not always time for individual interviews, and traditional employee satisfaction surveys do not inspire employees to share their thoughts very deeply.'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'Apprix''s solution is to integrate an employee satisfaction survey into the company''s e-learning course. If the e-learning course covers, for example, a Code of Compliance topic, suitable tasks can easily be tailored to fit. Through the tasks, employees almost inadvertently reveal how they behave in various everyday situations or how they personally relate to the company''s values. Our content producers listen carefully to client wishes and transform ordinary exam questions into a package that produces valuable data. In addition to multiple-choice and case-based tasks, plenty of open text field tasks are included. Questions are worded so that employees find it easy to share their thoughts. An open text field can often cause blank page anxiety, but topics broken down into sufficiently small pieces help the respondent think more deeply about the subject. This also makes responding easier.'

When training reveals which issues employees find challenging in terms of job satisfaction or their own competence, action can be taken. Change really is that simple.'
