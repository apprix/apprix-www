        text: 'The potential of eLearning'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'In the early 2000s, around the same time Apprix was founded, learning environments were mainly internal HR department pages whose training content consisted primarily of various PDF and PPT files. As such, these systems did not serve needs very well, as there was no easy way to verify whether trainees had learned and internalised the material. The systems also lacked channels through which learners could share their ideas and questions.'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'We already saw the potential of eLearning back then, but felt that the content was not functional at the time. The same content that had been used before was placed into new learning environments without considering the new context of use at all. Apprix was founded to address this need – we wanted to offer better and higher-quality eLearning content that would make even a dull topic into an interesting course.'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'We sought to make content more appealing through gamification. The best aspect of gamified courses was found to be engagement, and engaging courses have become the core of our business. Engagement can be utilised in all e-learning courses, even those that are not gamified.'

        text: 'Solutions for external target groups too'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'Quite soon, companies also developed a need to train external target groups, such as suppliers, maintenance workers or cleaners operating at their premises. This brought a need for user authentication, among other things. Over time, Apprix has developed many solutions for serving external target groups (such as reminders for expiring certifications) that can be flexibly and easily added to individual courses. This has become one of Apprix''s strengths – effective solutions also for training external target groups.'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'Nowadays, it is also very common for course completion data to be transferred between different systems (such as HR, LMS and access control systems). Various integrations have been implemented for several years, but demand has grown significantly over the past couple of years.'

        text: 'Currently on the rise: blended learning'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'In addition to integrations, combining e-learning with face-to-face content – so-called "blended learning" – is currently popular. For example, one of our clients uses e-learning courses to cover the theoretical parts. These are followed by an in-person training day where the focus can be on practical matters, since all participants already have a foundation in the subject.'
  -
    type: paragraph
    content:
      -
        type: text
        text: 'There is no single right way to create e-learning courses – the situation and needs determine the best approach in each case. As a general tip, however, I would suggest that you should engage the learner and always utilise practical examples in your courses.'
